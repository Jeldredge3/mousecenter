What to feed your pet mice

Mice are omnivores and can eat almost anything a human can. Like humans, they need a balanced and low calorie diet to keep healthy. Most Rat & Mouse pellet food brands you can get at your local pet store will be the best source of food for them, as they often contain a variety of grains and vitamins. Not all pellet food you find is safe for mice, however. Some package mixes contain food that is harmful and even poisonous even though a mouse might be featured on the packaging. Before purchasing a pellet food mix for rodents, make sure you're not selecting anything that contains unsafe foods for your mice.

Foods to avoid

Acidic or fatty foods should not be fed to your mice.

Poisonous foods include:

  • Oranges
  • Lemons
  • Grapefruit
  • Grapes
  • Raisins
  • Lettuce
  • Avacado
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Peanuts
  • Walnuts
  • Apple-seeds
  • Coffee
  • Raw Beans
  • Raw Meats
  • Bacon
  • Chocolate
  • Peppers
  • Spicy or Salty Foods

Foods that are healthy

Now you know what to avoid, there are plenty of nutrional options that are great for mice.

Healthy foods include:

  • Brocoli
  • Carrots
  • Cauliflower
  • Cellery
  • Cucumber
  • Peas
  • Apples
  • Apricots
  • Blueberries
  • Bananas
  • Mangos
  • Melons
  • Peaches
  • Pears
  • Plums
  • Coconut

When feeding your mice wet foods, such as a fresh banana or strawberry, be sure to not keep it in the cage too long, as they can quickly spoil and give your mice upset stomach.

Foods that are safe in small portions

There are many other foods that they enjoy, however, some should be reserved for special treats given in small portions once every few days or once a week. Too much fatty foods can lead to obesity and malnourishment in your mice.

Safe treat foods include:

  • Cereals (Cornflakes, Special K, Bran Flakes, Rice Krispies)
  • Biscuts
  • Breads
  • Boiled eggs
  • Cooked/dried pastas and rice
  • Cheese
  • Dog Biscuts
  • Fresh Mealworms
  • Oats
  • Sunflower seeds
